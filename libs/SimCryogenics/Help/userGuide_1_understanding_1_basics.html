1.1. Fluid helium basics 

A. Physical aspect

Fluid helium library is based on three variables :

  • M : the mass flow rate in [kg/s]
  • P : the pressure in [bar]
  • H : the enthalpy in [J/kg]
But for a better comprehension, the enthalpy is automatically converted into temperature (in Kelvin).

In this library, it's not information that travel across the model as it's the case in Simulink.
Here it's physical data : M, P and H.
And because of this, the model is called acasual. There is no real input and output in the blocks of this library, there is components that provides or receives physical data at they outlet/inlet.
All the aspects of physical modelling are the same than in the toolbox Simscape, please have a look at this link to learn mode about it.

B. Consequence on the system

The main consequence on the system is the fact that at the outlet or inlet of a block, all physical variables (M, P, H) have to be define.
So you have to be careful with the connection of your blocks : you can't connect two ports of two different blocks that provide the same physical variables.
In the same way, you can't connect a port that provides one physical variable to another one that also provides one physical variable (even if it's not the same),
because you have to define all the physical variables in this connection, and here you just define 2 of the 3 physical variables.
In the connection, the blocks have to be complementary in order to define all the physical variables.

As example, a compressor provides mass flow rate and temperature,(physical variables M and T) at his outlet.
And a pipe provides pressure (physical variable P) at his inlet. So the connection between the outlet of the compressor and the inlet of the pipe is good, because all the physical data are known at their ports.

To help you during the construction of your model, each components include a help that gives you the variables provide by this blocks and the ones required for the block.
